# Post-Drainage Care Instructions

## Wound Care
- Keep the dressing clean and dry.
- Change dressing daily or as instructed.
- Wash hands before and after touching the wound.
- Use sterile gauze and tape.

## Signs of Infection
Monitor for the following signs of infection:
- Increased pain or tenderness
- Erythema (redness) spreading beyond the wound edges
- Purulent drainage (yellow or green pus)
- Fever >100.4°F (38°C)
- Chills or malaise

If any of these signs occur, contact the clinic immediately.

## Activity Restrictions
- Avoid strenuous activity for 48 hours.
- Keep the affected area elevated if possible.
- Do not soak the wound (e.g., baths, swimming) until healed.

## Medication
- Take prescribed antibiotics as directed.
- Use over-the-counter pain relievers (acetaminophen, ibuprofen) as needed for pain.

## Follow-up
- Return for follow-up appointment in 2–3 days for packing removal.
- Call the office if you have any concerns.
